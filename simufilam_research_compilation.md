## CLINICAL TRIAL DATA

### RETHINK-ALZ (Phase 3, n=804, 52 weeks)
- **Co-primary endpoints: FAILED**
  - ADAS-Cog12: p=0.49
  - ADCS-ADL: p=0.72
- **Mild subgroup (MMSE 21-27):** ADAS-Cog12 Week 40 **p=0.02** — statistically significant
- **Biomarkers:** p-tau181 no change (p=0.23)

### REFOCUS-ALZ (Phase 3, n=1,125, 76 weeks)
- **Co-primary endpoints: FAILED**
  - ADAS-Cog12: p=0.36
  - ADCS-ADL: p=0.41
- **Mild subgroup signal (consistent across timepoints):**
  - ADAS-Cog12 Week 4: **p=0.01**
  - ADAS-Cog12 Week 28: **p=0.015**
  - ADAS-Cog12 Week 40: **p=0.02**
- **P-tau181 ≥67 pg/mL pooled mild subgroup:**
  - Week 4: **p=0.001**
  - Week 28: **p=0.01**

### THE 21% — UNRESOLVED QUESTION
- **21% of Phase 3 participants were AMYLOID-NEGATIVE**
- The Phase 3 paper states: plasma p-tau181 entry criterion "did not reliably exclude amyloid PET negativity"
- This suggests a screening failure — patients without confirmed AD pathology were enrolled
- **HOWEVER:** The paper does NOT definitively state whether they were negative at baseline or became negative during treatment
- No amyloid PET was required at enrollment — only plasma p-tau181 OR prior amyloid PET
- **Don's hypothesis:** These patients may have cleared amyloid during treatment (evidence of efficacy)
- **Published interpretation:** Screening criterion was unreliable (enrollment flaw)
- **CRITICAL:** This needs definitive resolution. If amyloid PET data exists for these patients at baseline AND during/after treatment, it would settle the question
- Either way, including non-AD patients dilutes the primary endpoint analysis
- **ACTION ITEM:** Aaron/Rascal — find whether baseline amyloid PET data exists for REFOCUS-ALZ participants. This is the key question.

### Phase 2 (Open-label)
- 47% of patients showed cognitive IMPROVEMENT (not slowing — actual reversal)
- Zero serious adverse events
- This was the signal that drove Phase 3

## THE HONEST NARRATIVE

## THE HONEST NARRATIVE

1. A drug showed genuine cognitive improvement in Phase 2 (47% improved, zero serious adverse events)
2. Phase 3 ran two large trials — primary endpoints failed on full population
3. 21% of participants were amyloid-negative — whether from screening failure or treatment effect is UNRESOLVED
4. Mild subgroup showed slower decline through Week 64 (p=0.019) but signal disappeared at Week 76 with 45% missing data
5. REFOCUS mild subgroup: ADAS-Cog12 signal at Weeks 4 (p=0.01), 28 (p=0.015), 40 (p=0.02)
6. P-tau181 ≥67 pg/mL pooled mild: Week 4 p=0.001, Week 28 p=0.01
7. Mild subgroup did NOT replicate across both trials consistently
8. Before a properly designed biomarker-selected trial could be run, short-sellers destroyed the company
9. SEC found disclosure violations, NOT scientific fraud. DOJ dropped ALL charges WITH PREJUDICE (Oct 2025).
10. The drug's mechanism has independent validation (PMID:41754821, 2026)

**The data is mixed but NOT negative.** There is real signal in the mild subgroup with biomarker confirmation. The 21% question could strengthen or weaken the case — but no one investigated it because the program was killed. The crime is that the experiment was never allowed to finish properly.

## WHAT WOULD NEED TO HAPPEN

4. **Primary endpoint redesign:** Include cognitive IMPROVEMENT as a possible outcome, not just slowing decline
5. **Independent sponsor:** Academic medical center, not Cassava (credibility damaged)
6. **Funding:** NIH, philanthropic, or pharma partnership
7. **FDA engagement:** Pre-IND meeting framing amyloid clearance as evidence of mechanism, not exclusion criterion
