## Part 1: What Is Simufilam?

Simufilam is a small molecule drug that targets **filamin A**, a protein that becomes altered in Alzheimer's disease. Unlike amyloid-targeting antibodies (Leqembi, Aduhelm) that cost $26K+/year and require IV infusion, simufilam is an **oral pill** — cheap to manufacture, easy to take.

The mechanism: altered filamin A causes a cascade of downstream problems in the brain — tau hyperphosphorylation, neuroinflammation, amyloid accumulation. Simufilam binds filamin A and restores its normal shape, addressing the root cause rather than just clearing amyloid plaque.

**Source:** [PubMed — Filamin A reversal study (PMID:37762230)](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/37762230/)

## Part 3: The Contaminated Trials

Cassava ran two Phase 3 trials:

- **RETHINK-ALZ** (NCT04994483) — 100mg vs placebo, 804 patients, 52 weeks
- **REFOCUS-ALZ** (NCT05026177) — 50mg vs 100mg vs placebo, 1,125 patients, 76 weeks

### The Screening Problem

To confirm patients actually had Alzheimer's, the trials used a **plasma p-tau181 blood test** instead of the gold standard amyloid PET scan. This was the first time Phase 3 AD trials relied primarily on a plasma biomarker for biological confirmation.

An amyloid PET sub-study within REFOCUS revealed the problem:

> **"21% of participants (33 of 160) were unexpectedly amyloid negative at baseline, indicating an absence of Alzheimer's disease pathology."**

One in five patients in the trial **didn't have Alzheimer's disease.** The blood test wasn't reliable enough to screen them out.

The published paper confirms:

> "The plasma p-tau181 entry criterion did not reliably exclude amyloid PET negativity in the sub-study."

**Think about what this means for the results.** If 21% of your treatment group doesn't have the disease, they can't improve from a drug that treats the disease. They're noise that dilutes the signal. This is like testing a cancer drug on a group where 1 in 5 patients don't have cancer, then declaring the drug "failed."

## Part 4: They Killed the Trial Early

RETHINK reported topline results in **November 2024** — missed endpoints. RETHINK used the same p-tau181 screening criterion as REFOCUS. The PET sub-study was only in REFOCUS, but both trials used the same unreliable blood test for enrollment — the contamination problem likely affected both.

What happened next: Cassava **terminated REFOCUS early** on November 25, 2024 — the dose-comparison trial — based on RETHINK's miss. **22% of REFOCUS patients were still active in the trial.**

REFOCUS was a **76-week study**. It was collecting:
- Amyloid PET scans at baseline AND week 76
- Tau PET scans
- CSF biomarkers
- MRI brain volumes
- P-tau217, GFAP, neurofilament light chain

**The trial was terminated with 22% of patients still active, meaning not all patients reached week 76.** But for patients who did — the amyloid PET scans, tau PET scans, and biomarker data that would show whether the drug cleared amyloid in confirmed-AD patients have **never been reported.** The protocol called for baseline-to-week-76 imaging. Whatever was collected before termination has not been published.

The open-label extension (NCT05575076) where 1,081 patients were being monitored long-term? **Terminated January 2025** by "sponsor decision." Safety-only — no biomarker data was collected.
