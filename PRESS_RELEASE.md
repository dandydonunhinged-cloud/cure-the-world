## Independent Researcher Uses Recursive AI Engine to Generate Novel Cancer Treatment Protocols — 6 AI Models Verify Novelty, 5 Produce Corrected Protocols After Adversarial Demolition

### Framework targets cancer's ability to adapt rather than individual tumor outputs; minimum viable protocol uses exclusively FDA-approved drugs

---

**DALLAS, TX** — Donald Brown, an independent systems researcher with no medical training, has published a comprehensive set of cancer treatment protocols generated through a novel recursive cognitive methodology called RPCCP (Recursive Precision Cognitive Calibration Protocol). The protocols, verified by six independent frontier AI models, propose a paradigm shift in cancer-associated fibroblast (CAF) therapeutics: targeting the tumor microenvironment's *ability to adapt* rather than its individual outputs.

The full protocols and supporting data are available at: **https://changethenarrative.click**

### The Discovery

Over 17 sequential RPCCP runs conducted between March 12-17, 2026, the recursive engine produced eight distinct therapeutic protocols addressing cancer-associated fibroblasts from multiple angles:

- **Stromal Capacity Collapse Protocol (SCCP):** Uses exclusively FDA-approved or commercially available drugs (phenformin, itacitinib, galunisertib, pembrolizumab, plerixafor) in a novel sequence that disables CAF adaptive capacity through SIRT1 inhibition, then forces clean apoptosis rather than pro-tumorigenic senescence.

- **Stromal Extinction Protocol (SEP):** A defense-in-depth approach with three independent attack layers yielding a joint resistance probability below 10^-15 per cell.

- **Stromal Attractor Inversion Protocol (SAIP):** A gene therapy approach that turns the tumor's own TGF-beta signaling into a stromal self-destruct trigger — becoming *stronger* as the tumor grows.

### The Convergence

Perhaps the most striking finding emerged from Runs 8-10: when the engine optimized for *maximum immune engagement* and separately optimized for *minimum toxicity*, both optimization paths converged on the same patient experience — transient flu-like symptoms lasting 7-14 days.

"The side effects aren't side effects," Brown explains. "Fever, fatigue, mild nausea — these are the vital signs of a restored immune system doing what it evolved to do. Cancer patients don't experience these symptoms because tumor stroma suppresses immune function. When you remove the suppression, the body responds."

### Adversarial Verification

In a novel application of multi-model adversarial testing, the protocols were submitted to five independent frontier AI models (NVIDIA Nemotron-3 Super 120B, Kimi K2 Thinking, Qwen 3.5 397B, Cogito 2.1 671B, and DeepSeek V3.2) for demolition. All five models unanimously identified eight fatal flaws in one protocol variant (SEPC).

When those eight objections were fed back as constraints — "How do you fix each of these problems and STILL achieve the desired outcome?" — each model independently produced a corrected protocol. The five corrected protocols converged on specific therapeutic targets:

- **5/5 models:** Abandon signal withdrawal; kill CAFs directly
- **5/5 models:** Use FAP enzymatic activity as targeting gate
- **5/5 models:** Small molecules over fusion proteins
- **3/5 models:** Target MCL-1 (not BCL-2) for CAF apoptosis
- **3/5 models:** Poly-ICLC (TLR3 agonist) for controlled immune activation

### The Methodology

RPCCP is a seven-pass recursive cognitive engine that forces AI systems through iterative self-correction under human-imposed constraints. Three critical human corrections transformed the engine's output from abstract philosophy (Run 1) to specific therapeutic protocols (Runs 4-6):

1. **Anchor Requirement:** Prevents conceptual drift across passes
2. **Solution Gate:** Forces each pass to produce actionable solutions, not analyses
3. **Failure-as-Constraint:** Each run's identified failures become hard constraints for the next run

"Without these corrections, the engine produced philosophy," Brown says. "With them, it produced testable therapeutic protocols. The corrections are the intellectual property — not the AI."
