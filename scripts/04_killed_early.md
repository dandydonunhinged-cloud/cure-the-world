# EXPLAINER 4 — "They Killed It Before the Data Could Speak"
# Target: 3-4 minutes
# Topic: Early termination, unreleased data, open-label extension shutdown

---

## SCRIPT

RETHINK reported topline results in November 2024. Missed endpoints. The headline: simufilam failed.

But we just showed you — 21% contamination from non-Alzheimer's patients. A blood test that didn't work. Despite that, signal in the confirmed patients.

What happened next is the part that should keep you up at night.

On November 25, 2024, Cassava terminated REFOCUS. The dose-comparison trial. The one with 1,125 patients. The one running for 76 weeks.

Twenty-two percent of REFOCUS patients were still active in the trial.

They didn't let it finish.

REFOCUS wasn't just a drug-versus-placebo study. It was collecting comprehensive biomarker data: amyloid PET scans at baseline AND at week 76. Tau PET scans. CSF biomarkers. MRI brain volumes. P-tau217. GFAP. Neurofilament light chain.

The amyloid PET scans at week 76 would answer the most important question in this entire story: did the drug clear amyloid from the brains of patients who were confirmed to have it at baseline?

That data was being collected. Some of it was already collected. And it has never been published.

The press release confirming the week-76 imaging protocol is public. It says the study included "an evaluation of various brain volumes using MRI and amyloid and tau deposition using PET scans from baseline to week 76."

The data exists. It's sitting in Cassava's files. No one has seen it.

Then came the open-label extension — trial NCT05575076. This is where patients from both Phase 3 trials could continue receiving simufilam and be monitored for long-term safety. 1,081 patients enrolled.

On January 31, 2025, Cassava terminated it. Reason listed on ClinicalTrials.gov: "sponsor decision."

630 patients were terminated because the sponsor pulled the plug. Not because of safety. Not because of adverse events. The sponsor decided to stop.

300 patients completed. 71 withdrew on their own. 24 had adverse events. But 630 were simply... cut off.

And the extension collected only safety data. No biomarkers. No imaging. We'll never know if those 1,081 patients were improving, stable, or declining — because nobody measured it.

Let me say this plainly: a drug that showed signal in confirmed Alzheimer's patients had its trials terminated early, its long-term monitoring shut down, and its most critical imaging data locked away — all while the company was being bled dry by lawsuits, SEC penalties, and a short-selling campaign the FDA itself said was improper.
